Dungeon where you make use of a new [Secret Skill](/system/secret-skills) gameplay system to progress.<br/>Secret skills are learned by reading skill scrolls, and spirit charges as turns elapse or you take damage.<br/>Skill scrolls can be found in this dungeon, and you can bring any number of skill scrolls when entering.<br/>Allies are also allowed, so keep that in mind if you're struggling to clear it on your own.

It might seem counterintuitive, but intentionally taking damage can be an effective way to charge spirit.<br/>Islands with items are pretty common, and can be reached using the <span class="strong">Strider</span> skill.

### General

See [Secret Skills](/system/secret-skills) for an overview about the Secret Skill gameplay system.

#### Story Clear (16F)

The story clear is on the easier side, but you'll need to know how to collect arrows to have a smooth run.<br/>Ideally, you'll also want a skill that deals damage to multiple enemies in case you encounter a Monster House.<br/>But generally speaking, you'll likely be fine if you have arrows and the <span class="strong">Power Bow</span> skill.

Arrow traps are common, so throw items onto arrow traps from 10 tiles away to collect arrows.<br/>If there isn't enough distance, you can dig out wall tiles using <span class="strong">Rock Break</span> or <span class="strong">Rock Crush</span>.<br/>Alternatively, use <span class="strong">Dodge</span> on a Boy Cart (8-10F) floor to easily collect 99 Iron Arrows.

When descending to the next floor, it's best to have full HP and spirit meter, and at least 50% fullness.<br/>It's best to charge up 1 spirit before entering hallways so that you have access to <span class="strong">Precise Sword</span>,<br/>and use said skill instead of a normal direct attack against monsters with troublesome specials.

It's easy to forget, but the dungeon allows allies, so bring Jin or Minamo along if you're struggling.

#### Full Version (99F)

The 2nd clear is significantly harder - It's recommended to master most skills before attempting it.<br/>You can easily master skills by equipping a Spirit Bracelet + Gold Sword + Gold Shield (with Otogiriso seals),<br/>followed by bringing skill scrolls to Tenrin Easy and intentionally getting hit by monster attacks.<br/>※ Spirit charges slower in Kron Trial compared to Soaring Bird's Challenge (鳥飛魔天の挑戦).

The ideal pace is to obtain Bamboo Sword, Decoy Bracelets, Gold Sword, and Gold Shield by 10F.<br/>Once you've obtained a Bamboo Sword, swing the weapon until the wind blows to increase max strength.<br/>This is usually enough to have a smooth time until Devil Kangaroo (41-50, 61-70F) floors.<br/>Ironhead's Head helps conserve arrows and prevents surprise attacks in hallways.

Rush stairs on Devil Kangaroo floors - You can level up and restock items after those floors.<br/>Intentionally collapsing with a Decoy Bracelet equipped can be an advantage since you revive with \~10 spirit.<br/>An example would be using <span class="strong">Keen Sight</span> to look for a Pitfall or <span class="strong">Blind</span> to buy some time in a Monster House.<br/>Decoy Bracelets aren't too rare if you use <span class="strong">Steal</span> or <span class="strong">Sumo Stamp</span> to obtain items.

#### List

Number in parenthesis is the skill's spirit cost.

- Deep Breathing (5)
    - Lets you win most 1 vs 1 fights as long as the monster doesn't have a special attack.
- Air Meal (6)
    - Replenishes 30 fullness.
- Precise Sword (1)
    - Perform a direct attack with 100% accuracy, even against a Pumphantasm type inside a wall.
- Power Bow (1)
    - Shoot an arrow with double power - It often deals more damage than a direct attack.
- Rock Break (1)
    - Digs out a wall tile in front of you.
- Ghost Slash (3)
    - Deals x5.0 damage to Ghost types, and can be used to one-shot Ghost Warrior.
- Drain Slash (3)
    - Deals x5.0 damage to Drain types.
- Keen Sight (10)
    - Reveals hidden traps and Ether Devil types.
- Purify (10)
    - Removes curses from all items in your inventory.
- Disarm (10)
    - Knocks away a weapon from an enemy - Aim for Bamboo Sword, Gold Sword, and Bladed Wheel.
- Nullify Blast (2)
    - Makes you immune to explosion damage - Great against Pop Tank types.
- Zen (6)
    - Makes you immune to fire damage - Essential for Archdragon floors.
- Nullify Lightning (2)
    - Makes you immune to electric damage - Pud types, avoid damage when using Electric Sword, etc.
- Electric Sword (3)
    - Attack that travels through all adjacent creatures, similar to Lightning Staff.
- Wild Dance (4)
    - Doubles your attack power at the exchange of 0 defense for 10 turns.
- Strider (5)
    - Lets you walk on water or lava tiles for a little while.
- Silk Palm (5)
    - Puts an enemy in front of you to sleep for 5 turns.
- Slumber Scent (10)
    - Same effect as Slumber Scroll - Great for rushing stairs in a Monster House.
- Thunder (5) or Rockslide (6)
    - Same effect as Lightning Scroll or Rockfall Scroll.
- Dodge (6)
    - Dodge all projectiles aimed at you for the duration of the current floor.
    - Makes collecting arrows from Boy Carts trivial.
- Flee (5)
    - Warp to a different room on the current floor.
- Steal (7)
    - Steal an item from an enemy - Derived from <span class="strong">Walrus Guard</span>.
- Sumo Stamp (10)
    - Makes all monsters in the room drop an item.
- Blind (8)
    - Inflicts Blind status on all monsters in the room.

### Equipment

#### Weapon

- Bamboo Sword
    - It takes a long time, but raising max strength to 99 is very helpful for the 2nd clear.
- Bladed Wheel
    - Use it as a projectile after raising max strength to conserve arrows.
    - A cursed Bladed Wheel can be used with a Spoil Trap to obtain Spoiled Onigiri.
- Gold Sword
    - Pair it with a Gold Shield to activate a resonance effect that prevents bracelet damage.
- Ironhead's Head
    - Being able to hit enemies before they're adjacent is powerful.

#### Shield

- Frontal Shield
    - Win most 1 vs 1 fights from the front, but be careful not to get hit from the sides or back.
    - Keep an escape item like Flee Scroll on hand in case you get surrounded.
- Gold Shield
    - Pair it with a Gold Sword to activate a resonance effect that prevents bracelet damage.

#### Bracelets

- Protect Bracelet
    - Shop exclusive - Reduces damage received by 25%.
- Decoy Bracelet
    - Revives you if you collapse - Keep it equipped on Devil Kangaroo floors.

### Other Items

Some of the other notable items.

- Storage Pot
    - Shop exclusive - Always buy any of these that you find in a shop.
- Heal Pot
    - Keep 2\~3 on hand to have peace of mind.
    - However, be aware that Gazer has the possibility of making you insert key items.
- Revival Grass
    - Obtained from Custard.
- Arrows
    - Rocks and Poison Arrows are handy, but prioritize Wood and Iron arrows since they're more common.
    - Store arrows in Storage Pots to protect them from getting cursed.
- Paralysis Staff, Seal Staff
    - Dropped by King Tusker and Monarch Tusker.
    - Lets you deal with furious monsters on Devil Kangaroo floors without having to spend spirit.
- Sight Scroll, Swift Scroll, Flee Scroll
    - Nice to have in case you start the floor in a Monster House.

# Floor Guide

This section is mostly written with the 2nd clear in mind.<br/>You don't really need to farm items or grind if you're just aiming for the story clear.

### 1-2F

Monsters are weak, so you shouldn't have problems.<br/>It's fine to read skill scrolls if you haven't learned or mastered the skill yet, but otherwise save them for later.<br/>Reading a skill scroll performs the skill without spending spirit, and duplicate scrolls can be sold.

If you're aiming for the 2nd clear, it's best to master <span class="strong">Disarm</span> and <span class="strong">Steal</span> beforehand so that you can collect<br/>items like Bamboo Sword, Bladed Wheel, Gold Sword, and Decoy Bracelet on these floors.<br/>Charge spirit faster by intentionally getting hit by monster attacks.

### 3-8F

Traps can be generated starting on 3F.<br/>If you find a Monster Trap, spread items around and turn them into monsters, then use <span class="strong">Sumo Stamp</span>.<br/>A Trip Stone + cursed Bladed Wheel or <span class="strong">Soft Pitch</span> or <span class="strong">Spirit Throw</span> can also be used to obtain items.

Use <span class="strong">Dodge</span> to collect Wood Arrows from Bow Boy (4-5F) and Iron Arrows from Boy Cart (8-10F).<br/>If you have <span class="strong">Nullify Blast</span>, create a Cranky Tank and defeat it to level up.

If aiming for the 2nd clear, it's recommended to raise max fullness to 200 using Nigiri Morph (6-8F).<br/>Frontal Shield or <span class="strong">Deep Breathing</span> can be used instead of Poison Arrows.

### Shichiten City

Buy P.Bow Scroll, Precise Scroll, and Meal Scroll if you haven't learned those skills yet.<br/>※ The item shop will become unavailable during the final part of the main story.

### 9-10F

Continue collecting Iron Arrows from Boy Cart (8-10F) and hunting Cranky Tanks if possible.

Keep swinging Bamboo Sword to raise max strength while using <span class="strong">Steal</span> to obtain skill scrolls.<br/>If you don't have Bamboo Sword yet, use <span class="strong">Disarm</span> instead.

### 11-16F

Have Spike Dragon (11-13F) defeat other enemies and turn into a Thunder Lance, then defeat it to level up.<br/>Hannya Warrior (13-17F) and Dragon (15-20F) overlap, so you could try for an Archdragon as well.<br/>However, in that case you'll need the <span class="strong">Zen</span> skill and lots of projectiles.

### Boss

Fight against Aquila.

|Name|HP|Atk|Def|Exp|Notes|
|-|-|-|-|-|-|
|Aquila|100|20|5|3000|・Tries to stay at least 2 tiles away from Asuka.<br/>・Does a knockback attack when adjacent.<br/>・Summons Whirlwinds.|
|Whirlwind|5|3|1|6|・Converts non-fixed damage attacks to 1 damage.<br/>・Does a knockback attack when adjacent, then vanishes.<br/>・Reflects projectiles.|

Aquila and Whirlwinds both have attacks that deal knockback, so it's difficult to approach Aquila.<br/>Projectiles are reflected by Whirlwinds but are effective against Aquila, so the easiest strategy is to<br/>shoot about 3 Iron Arrows while using the <span class="strong">Power Bow</span> skill to quickly defeat the boss at the start.

#### Spirit Bracelet

If you return to this room after clearing the dungeon, you can obtain a Spirit Bracelet.<br/>There will be 2 exits - One is used to return to Yakagura Forest, and the other continues to the 2nd clear.

### 17-25F

Dazikon (15-19F), King Tusker (15-19F), Twisty Hani (19-21F), and Bat Kangaroo (19-21F) appear.<br/>19F is particularly dangerous since all of the above overlap, and there's risk of a Twisty Dance party occurring.

You shouldn't struggle if you're at 99 strength, but you'll start to encounter enemies you can't one-shot soon.

### 26-30F

Evil Kangaroo (26-30F) appears, and has a high chance of being generated.<br/>Triple speed Minotaurs can quickly reduce your HP to 0, so it might be best to rush stairs.<br/>Frontal Shield helps, but be aware that swift monsters can sneak up behind you in hallways.

### 31-40F

Bat Kangaroo types don't appear on these floors, so it's relatively safe.<br/>Stock up on skill scrolls, Decoy Bracelets, and shop exclusive items prior to the dreaded 41-50F floor range.

Shogun (31-40F), Earth Needle (31-50F), and Sky Dragon (25-35F) appear, so hunt some Lv3 monsters to level up.<br/>If you plan on lingering for a while, use <span class="strong">Keen Sight</span> to avoid surprise attacks by Phantom Devil (31-50F).

### 41-50F

Devil Kangaroo (41-50F) and Nigiri Master (41-60F) overlap - <span class="redText">Devil Kangaroo has a high spawn rate.</span><br/>You're very likely to encounter furious monsters, so keep a Decoy Bracelet equipped and rush stairs.<br/>Some players prefer to stay in a room until the wind blows to gain the Navigation Scroll effect before exploring.<br/>Elec Bomb can be used to block off a hallway, but keep in mind that Tiger Hurler can throw the Elec Bomb.

If you're worried about getting sandwiched in a hallway, use <span class="strong">Rock Crush</span> to dig a path instead.

### 51-60F

Vexing Kappa (51-65F), Tiger Chucker (56-65F), Custard (56-70F), and Venom Sluggy (56-70F) appear.<br/>It's safer than the previous floor range, but some of these enemy special attacks can be troublesome.<br/>Don't forget to use <span class="strong">Dodge</span> in addition to <span class="strong">Keen Sight</span> to avoid getting hit by items thrown by Vexing Kappa.<br/>Use <span class="strong">Power Bow</span> to defeat Venom Sluggy in one hit to prevent it from multiplying.

Custard has a \~3.5% chance to drop Revival Grass when defeated, so <span class="redText">don't</span> use <span class="strong">Steal</span> on it.<br/>It's best to hunt them between 56-60F, since Devil Kangaroo appears again from 61F.

### 61-70F

Mirage Devil (61-65F), Devil Kangaroo (61-70F), Fire Bomb (61-70F), Porkon (61-75F), Sonic Boar (61-75F),<br/>Hyper Gazer (66-75F), Sludge (66-80F), etc.

The nightmare of Devil Kangaroo returns - <span class="redText">Devil Kangaroo has a high spawn rate.</span><br/>Keep a Decoy Bracelet equipped at all times, and beware of item loss due to Sonic Boar's tripping attack.<br/>Balance Staff, Desert Scroll, and Great Hall Scroll can't be found, so items in water can't be retrieved.

Use <span class="strong">Dodge</span> to avoid rocks thrown by Porkons, and consider blocking off a hallway using Fire Bomb<br/>or using Sludge as a wall and waiting until the wind blows if you're having a hard time with surprise attacks.<br/>Try your best to descend the stairs with 10 spirit charged up.

### 71-99F

Astral Devil (71-80, 91-99F) and Dozikon (76-90F) appear.<br/>Use <span class="strong">Keen Sight</span> at the start of a floor to reveal Astral Devils, but prioritize <span class="strong">Dodge</span> if you see a Dozikon.<br/>If you didn't have enough spirit and got attacked by an invisible Astral Devil, use <span class="strong">Counter</span> to reflect its attacks.<br/>Even if you don't defeat it, Asuka will face the Astral Devil, so you can finish it off with <span class="strong">Precise Sword</span>.

81-90F is the final floor range where you can safely restock items - Don't forget to use <span class="strong">Dodge</span> first.<br/>Archdragon (86-99F)'s fire breath isn't much of a threat if you have the <span class="strong">Zen</span> skill, so the main threat is Astral Devil.
