Regular Weapons

Wooden Sword (きたえた木刀)

Low attack, but comes with an upgrade value ranging from +4 to +8.
However, it can be found with an upgrade value of -1 if it's cursed.

Mostly used as a secondary item in synthesis to strengthen a stronger weapon,
since base attack is far more important than upgrade value in this game.
Therefore it's a bit lackluster if you find it in dungeons that don't allow carry-in items.

Equipping a Wooden Shield raises its base attack by 3, but it's still impractical
compared to other weapons since it starts with only 2 base attack and 1 seal slot.

Resonance
Wooden Shield (+3 base attack and defense)

Club (こんぼう)

Basic weapon that has been in every game starting with Shiren 1.
You can count on finding this weapon first thanks to its high generation chance.

Commonly treated as the weakest weapon in other series, and this game is no exception.
Since it's made of wood, it won't rust, but can instead lose upgrade value by rotting
due to Rust Trap or Slime family monster special attacks.

It has 3 synthesis slots, so it can be used as a base when making Healing Sword.
However, it's a strictly worse option than the commonly found Nagamaki.

Nagamaki (長巻)

Similar to Club, this weapon has a high chance of being generated.
Commonly used as an interim weapon since it lacks an innate ability.

Keep seal order in mind when using this weapon as a base weapon
rather than a Healing Sword ingredient. (seals transfer from left to right)

Dotanuki (どうたぬき)

Weapon made by the famous swordsmith Dotanuki Genma.
Doesn't have an innate ability, but is among the upper ranks of base weapon options.
It may not be the strongest, but it's plenty to help fight with consistency.

Manji-Kabura is the noteworthy upgrade to this weapon, but is extremely rare.
On the other hand, Dotanuki has a low but realistic chance of being generated,
so Dotanuki is typically the ideal base weapon in dungeons without carry-in items.

Amano Kagura (天神楽ノ剣)

If you lose this weapon, it can be found either on the same floor of the dungeon
that you collapsed in, or in the room to the right on the deepest floor of Firework Cannon.
This is the case even when the weapon is lost due to a Kengo family monster
disarming you, or if the weapon is inserted into a Presto Pot.

As an end game weapon, it doesn't have as high attack power as Fiery Fuuma
or Kaburasutegi, but it's still plenty for most dungeons that allow carry-in items.
It also has the highest default synthesis slot count among all one-handed weapons.

However, you'll often have valuable unbreakable bracelets or expanded Storage Pots
when you explore dungeons at end game, so it's a given that you won't collapse.
It's also possible to prevent equipment loss by synthesizing the 帰 seal (Retreat Scroll),
so it might not see much use overall.

It's not mentioned in the in-game description, but this weapon cannot be used
as a secondary item in synthesis. There's a glitch that spawns a second Amano Kagura
if you collapse in a dungeon after the weapon's seals change within a dungeon.

How to Obtain
Found in the room to the right on the deepest floor of Firework Cannon.
If you lose the weapon, it can be found in the same room again.

Fiery Fuuma (火迅風魔刀)

Weapon made by honing a Katana that has been upgraded to the limit.
The Katana must have an upgrade value of +99, and all seal slots must be filled.

It features higher base attack than Amano Kagura, but only has 4 synthesis slots.
It has equipment resonance with Helix Shield, but the effect is somewhat lackluster,
simply increasing base attack and defense by 5.

If you plan on using it, you'll want to synthesize some Fuuma Stones
to increase the number of synthesis slots.

How to Obtain
Hone a Katana+99 with all seal slots filled at the blacksmith.

Kaburasutegi (秘剣カブラステギ)

Mythical sword that not even the legendary Kabura could make consistently.
Strongest weapon in the game, with no others coming close to its raw attack power.
Dark Flame's Challenge is the only dungeon without carry-in items in which this
weapon can be made, so it's mostly restricted to dungeons with carry-in items.

It's made by synthesizing 8 specific seals onto a Manji-Kabura.
Manji-Kabura normally only has 6 slots, so you either synthesize Fuuma Stones
to increase the slot count, or clear Koga's Hidden Hole to obtain one with 8 slots.

Helix Shield also requires 8 slots, so it's best clear Koga's Hidden Hole to obtain
a Manji-Kabura or Fuuma Shield with 8 slots to use as a base,
and save Fuuma Stones for the other piece of equipment.

The DC version of Koga's Hidden Hole always rewards you with a Manji-Kabura,
while the PC version offers a random selection among Manji-Kabura,
Fuuma Shield, and Kengo's Katana.

How to Obtain
Synthesize 竜, 月, 目, 水, 回, 仏, 捨, 木 onto a Manji-Kabura. (Seal order doesn't matter)
Manji-Kabura has a default slot count of 6 slots, so either synthesize 2 Fuuma Stones,
or use an 8 slot Manji-Kabura obtained from clearing Koga's Hidden Hole.

Ghost Sickle (成仏のカマ)

Weapon that deals x1.5 damage to Ghost type enemies.
Ignores Ghost Warrior's ability that converts all damage received to 1 point.
Equipment resonance with Beast Shield, granting +30 damage to Ghost types.

Ghost types include Pumphantasm, Ghost Radish, Ether Devil, Bone Dragon,
Jagan Mask families, and Ghost Warrior. (6 families, 17 monsters)

These monsters can be a hassle, so it's among the useful type effective seals.
However, you still want to prevent Ghost Radish monsters from getting close to you,
and Ether Devil monsters can be difficult to hit without knowing where they're located.

It lacks base attack, but has 5 synthesis slots so it can be used as a main weapon,
especially if you can activate its equipment resonance with Beast Shield.
Katana has 8 attack and 5 slots, and Drain Buster has 6 attack and 5 slots.
Which of these you use mostly comes down to personal preference.

Resonance
Beast Shield (+30 damage to Ghost types)

Marine Slasher (マリンスラッシャー)

Weapon that deals x1.5 damage to Water type enemies.
Also allows you to hit underwater monsters using regular attacks.
Equipment resonance with Beast Shield, granting +30 damage to Water types.

Water types include Zaloklefts, Bored Kappa, Spearfish, and Pull Frog families.
(4 families, 13 monsters)

The only one you'd want to defeat in a single turn using regular attacks is Zalokleft,
since the special attacks of others aren't very threatening once you're next to them.
Zaloklefts don't appear in Reverse White Snake Isle, so there's little reason
to synthesize this seal inside that dungeon.

The ability to hit underwater monsters doesn't see much use, but it might
be a candidate for a weapon used in dungeons that allow carry-in items.

Cyclops Killer (1ツ目殺し)

Weapon that deals x1.5 damage to One-eyed type enemies.

Drain Buster (ドレインバスター)

Weapon that deals x1.5 damage to Drain type enemies.
Equipment resonance with Beast Shield, granting +30 damage to Drain types.

Drain types include Slime, Slug, Laughing Polygon families, and Twisty Hani.
(4 families, 13 monsters)

Note that Twisty Hani doesn't appear outside of Drain Houses in White Snake Isle
and Reverse White Snake Isle, so it's 3 families, 12 monsters in those dungeons.

Higher level Slime and Slug monsters can be problematic if they divide,
and Laughing Polygon's warp can be tricky to deal with, so it's among
the best type effective seals alongside Dragon Killer and Ghost Sickle.

However, it's safer unequip your weapon and shield when fighting Slime monsters,
and once you reach the deeper floors of a dungeon, Singing Polygon
is about the only monster left where this seal is effective.

Dragon Killer (ドラゴンキラー)

Weapon that deals x1.5 damage to Dragon type enemies.
Equipment resonance with Dragon Shield, granting +5 base attack and defense,
along with an additional +30 damage to Dragon types.
(Note that it doesn't resonate with Beast Shield)

Dragon types include Dragon, Mulon, Floor Dragon, Shaaga, Spike Dragon,
and Bone Dragon families. (6 families, 18 monsters)
It's effective against the highest number of targets among type effective weapons,
and Dragon types often have high attack and defense, so it's a nice seal to have.

It's also strong as a main weapon thanks to its base attack that surpasses Dotanuki.
With equipment resonance, its base attack increases to 20, surpassing Manji-Kabura.
The downside is that it only has 3 synthesis slots, but you can make up for it by
skipping 扇 seals if you find a Dragon Shield and use equipment resonance.

Early floors lack Dragon types, so it's most effective starting on middle floor ranges.

Special Ability Weapons

Sapping Branch (衰弱の枝)

Lowers a target's attack by 25%, and inflicts the sap status condition for 10 turns.
Sap status condition decreases HP every turn, like Dolly monster special attacks.
Stacking this seal only increases the amount of HP decrease per turn.
(The target's attack is lowered by 25% no matter how many seals you stack)

Easily one of the best seals in the game. Lowering a target's attack by 25% is an
extremely powerful effect, and the reduction applies each time you deal damage,
so as long as you can withstand the first few hits, you can restore HP almost
infinitely by equipping a shield with 弟 seals.

It allows you to hold your own in fights even if your shield is weak in the deepest
floors of a dungeon, including against enemies like Megataur and Archdragon.
However, situations where you're surrounded by monsters are still dangerous,
so don't get careless just because you have this seal.

The HP drain effect isn't as useful, but it can quickly defeat Ghost Warrior.
Note that you don't gain exp if you defeat monsters with the HP drain effect.

Pickaxe (つるはし)

Allows you to break wall tiles, excluding unbreakable walls.
It has limitless potential in terms of usefulness, depending on how you use it.

The enemy movement routine isn't predictable like Shiren 1 in Asuka Kenzan,
so it's harder use it in a calculated manner for throwing off enemies.
But it can still be used to create shortcuts, make it harder for enemies
to chase you, reach buried treasure rooms, etc.

It also allows you to safely wait for wind to blow, create terrain for hunting
Lv4 Slime monsters, control ally positions in Solidarity, and so on.

Razor Wind (妖刀かまいたち)

Allows you to attack in a 3-way pattern.
Weak as a main weapon with only 2 base attack, but excellent as a seal.
Can be used to quickly wipe out a Monster House, or get an extra hit in
by attacking around corners - which helps conserve arrows.

Plays well with Phantasm Bracelet and the 回 seal, allowing you to
safely clear out monster houses from inside a wall without losing HP.

Gamarra Whip (ガマラのムチ)

Whip that hosts the spirit of a Gamarra.
Defeated enemies have a 1/16 chance to drop Gitan.
The drop rate increases with multiple 銭 seals (n/16, n = number of seals)

It's typically not too difficult to come across Gitan inside dungeons,
and the drop rate + amount of Gitan is rather low, so the effect is a bit lackluster.
The weapon itself can be sold for 8000 Gitan, so it's usually best to just sell it.

Equipment resonance with Gamarra Shield, but it's rare to find both inside
a dungeon, and the effect simply adds 1 to your Gitan total every turn.
You start with both items inside the Free Economy dungeon,
but players usually sell both items right away.

Resonance
Gamarra Shield (Gain 1 Gitan per turn)

Bamboo Sword (根性の竹刀)

Sword that increases strength by 1 every time you swing the weapon 100 times.
Stacking 根 seals lowers the number of turns required to increase strength.
(100/n, n = number of seals)

This weapon essentially exists to help newcomers. As long as you have enough food,
you can easily increase your strength to 99, serving as a powerful balance breaker.
It's commonly found in the first story dungeon, Halo Country Easy, and can even
be found occasionally in the post-game dungeon White Snake Isle.
(However, it's not in the item table of Reverse White Snake Isle)

Simply swinging the weapon instead of stepping in place to recover HP will raise
strength by a decent amount, even if you don't intentionally linger on the current floor.
Can be synthesized onto your main weapon, but probably best as a secondary weapon.

Golden Sword (金の剣)

Luxurious sword made of solid gold.
Never rusts from Rust Trap or Slime monster special attacks.

As a seal, it allows you to fight Lv1~3 Slimes without unequipping your weapon.
Particularly nice for situations where you're surrounded by other monsters,
since you might not have the time to unequip the weapon or seal the Slime.
The blue 金 seal it provides cannot block Lv4 Slimes from erasing seals,
but unlike a red 金 seal (Plating Scroll), it will block the loss of upgrade value.

It's a bit weak with only 3 base attack, but its equipment resonance
with Golden Shield is possibly the best resonance effect in the game.
The effect completely prevents bracelets from shattering.
So depending on the bracelets you have, it can still be a great main weapon
option despite its low attack if you have a Golden Shield to go with it.

Aim for the Golden set if you find bracelets such as Phantasm, Thief, and Protect,
and be sure to synthesize multiple 扇 (Tessen) seals to increase your defense.

Resonance
Golden Shield (Bracelets never shatter)

Homing Blade (必中の剣)

Weapon that ensures your regular attacks will never miss.

Fantastic to have in situations where you can defeat a monster
with one more attack, but you will be the one to collapse if you miss,
or if you let a monster with a troublesome special attack move next to you.
Synthesizing it onto a Power Pole or Blast Hammer ensures you can defeat
monsters without taking damage, as long as they don't have a ranged attack.

It increases your expected damage output and is often compared to the 会 seal,
but the 必 seal increases damage over time rather than in a single turn.
It shines the most in situations where you can't afford to miss an attack.

It can be found in dungeons that don't allow carry-in items such as
White Snake Isle, but has a low chance of being generated.

Weapon that contains the power of a Nigiri Morph.
Defeated monsters have a 19% chance to drop a Riceball.
(Significantly higher chance than the 飯 seal, which starts at 3.1%)

Enemies can still drop other items, and the order of drops is Riceball → other items.
Keep that in mind when defeating pelican monsters near waterways.

One seal is enough to make hunger a non-issue, even with a Heavy Shield equipped.
It can rarely be found in dungeons like White Snake Isle and Reverse White Snake Isle.

Obtained as a clear reward for reaching 99F of Trial of Bufu. (DC verison only)

Dried Bonito (かつおぶし)

Weapon that can be bitten to restore 30% fullness using the [Bite] command.
Upgrade value decreases by 1 every time the weapon is bitten, and it can
no longer be bitten once the weapon strength decreases to 0.
Note that a cursed Dried Bonito cannot be bitten while it's equipped.

Synthesizing this weapon's seal allows you to bite the item used as a base.
It's best to synthesize the か seal onto weapons with high base attack, such as
Disposable Sword (35 atk, 1050% fullness) or Minotaur's Axe (20 atk, 600% fullness).

Some players prefer to synthesize this seal onto their main weapon instead of the
に seal for dungeons that allow carry-in items, since the number of Riceball drops
can slow down the pace of the game.

Bladed Wheel (車輪のやいば)

Sturdy weapon that isn't lost when it hits an enemy, but instead falls to the ground.
However, it will still burn up like other items if thrown at an Inferno.

Can be used as an infinite use projectile, and helps protect against item loss
due to Iai or Shihan special attacks if synthesized onto a main weapon.

It can also be thrown at a target who is standing on a trap to trigger the trap,
allowing you to collect items using Trip Stone or Spoil Trap without a Trapper Bracelet.
Be careful not to trigger traps like Landmine or Pitfall, since the weapon will be lost.

Healing Sword (回復の剣)

Weapon that restores HP equal to 1/3 of the amount of damage dealt.
Note that the 1/3 only applies to the amount of HP that was decreased.
(example: dealing 30 damage to a monster with 9 HP left will only restore 3 HP)
Obtained by synthesizing an Herb, Otogiriso, and Life Grass onto a weapon.

The ability to restore HP while attacking is fantastic, and plays well with seals
such as 会 (critical hits), 眠 (puts enemies to sleep), 衰 (lowers enemy attack),
and equipment like Frontal Shield.

It's difficult to make due to the required items and number of pelican monsters,
but it's well worth the cost, and is a key item to being able to clear post-game
dungeons like White Snake Isle and Reverse White Snake Isle.

Of course, it's also good to synthesize it onto your main weapon for dungeons
that allow carry-in items. (回 seals don't stack, so only synthesize one seal)

How to Obtain
Synthesize 薬, 弟, 命 onto any weapon. (Seal order doesn't matter)

Kengo's Katana (ケンゴウのカタナ)

Katana used by Kengo monsters.
Occasionally flings a weapon when performing a regular attack.
Enemy Kengo can disarm shields, but this weapon can only fling weapons.

Damage isn't dealt when the disarming effect activates, so you don't want
to use this weapon in situations where you need to defeat enemies quickly.

Avoid synthesizing it onto a main weapon, and since the effect occurs at random,
it's not a very good option for a secondary weapon either.
There usually isn't a need for other weapons by the time you obtain it,
so it generally doesn't see much use, and is mostly a collector's item.

Kengo monsters never drop this weapon, but it can be found in dungeon shops.
It doesn't appear in White Snake Isle, but it can be found in shops
starting on 70F in Reverse White Snake Isle.

A Kengo's Katana with 8 slots is one of the three possible clear rewards for
Koga's Hidden Hole (PC version), which is typically considered the worst reward.

Tessen (鉄扇)

Fan made of iron. Raises shield base defense by 3 when equipped.

Note that it's not "defense" or "upgrade value", but "base defense".
In this game, upgrade value is more potent the higher a shield's base defense is,
so increasing a shield's base defense by 3 is actually a very powerful effect.
You can't go wrong filling remaining slots with 扇 seals after the essentials.

It has decent attack but only 2 slots, so it's almost never used as a main weapon.
However, it's a nice interim option. (Synthesize seals like 金 or 会 in preparation)
Excels in early game before you've had a chance to find or strengthen a shield.

Wonder Pickaxe (サトリのつるはし)

Golden pickaxe that allows you to dig through walls, and never breaks.
It can be brought back upon clearing Trial of Gito or Odd Monkey's Challenge,
or created by combining 6 Pickaxes together using synthesis.

Starting equipment in dungeons where you have to dig your way through floors.
Choose between using it as a main weapon, or as a seal for a stronger weapon.
Many players have the impression that Pickaxes are weak weapons,
but Wonder Pickaxe is pretty solid with 8 base attack and 4 slots.
One advantage of using it as a main weapon is that you can avoid accidents
caused by Lv4 Slimes erasing the サ seal, resulting in the inability to progress.

How to Obtain
Synthesize 5 掘 seals onto a Pickaxe. (6 Pickaxes total)
Starting equipment in Trial of Gito and Odd Monkey's Challenge.

Disposable Sword (使い捨ての剣)

Sword that loses 1 upgrade value every time damage is dealt to an enemy.

It doesn't see much use as a weapon outside of early game Monster Houses.
Provides 30% * 35 = 1050% fullness if the か seal (Dried Bonito) is synthesized.
Can be used as a strong projectile if the 車 seal (Bladed Wheel) is synthesized.

This weapon's seal effect is completely negative, so don't carelessly synthesize it.
However, it's one of the required seals for creating a Kaburasutegi.
